This survey is part of a research study on communication gateway evolution for Software-Defined Vehicles (SDVs) conducted by Group 4 at the Vietnamese-German University (VGU), Smart Mobility Engineering program, Research Methodology course.

We are investigating how automotive gateway architectures are evolving — from traditional domain-based systems to zonal and centralized software-defined architectures — and what the key challenges are for OTA updates, safety, scalability, and Vietnam-specific deployment.

All responses are completely anonymous and will be used exclusively for academic research. Results will be aggregated — no individual response will be identified. Thank you for contributing your expertise.

📖 Key Terms — Quick Reference
SDV — Software-Defined Vehicle: a vehicle where software dynamically controls and updates core functions via a central gateway
Zonal Gateway — architecture grouping ECUs by physical location (zone) rather than function — reduces wiring, enables scalability
OTA — Over-The-Air update: wireless delivery of software updates to vehicle ECUs without physical access
Mixed-Criticality — running safety-critical (ASIL-D) and non-critical (infotainment) software on the same platform with isolation
